# üìã Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of level of experience, gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, personal appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or nationality.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

**We encourage behaviors that contribute to creating a welcoming and inclusive environment:**

### ü§ù **Collaboration and Respect**

- **Treat everyone** with respect, kindness, and empathy
- **Be inclusive** of different perspectives and experiences
- **Listen actively** and seek to understand before responding
- **Acknowledge mistakes** and learn from them
- **Give credit** where credit is due

### üéØ **Constructive Communication**

- **Provide constructive feedback** that helps others improve
- **Focus on what is best** for the community and healthcare patients
- **Show empathy** towards other community members
- **Be patient** with newcomers and those learning
- **Use welcoming and inclusive language**

### üí° **Professional Excellence**

- **Demonstrate expertise** while remaining approachable
- **Share knowledge** and mentor others when possible
- **Maintain high standards** for healthcare technology
- **Consider patient safety** and privacy in all discussions
- **Follow healthcare industry standards** and regulations

### üåç **Global Perspective**

- **Respect cultural differences** and international healthcare contexts
- **Be mindful** of time zones when scheduling meetings
- **Consider accessibility** needs in all communications
- **Accommodate language barriers** with patience and understanding
- **Value diverse healthcare systems** and practices

### Unacceptable Behavior

**We do not tolerate behaviors that exclude, intimidate, or harm others:**

### üö´ **Harassment and Discrimination**

- **Unwelcome comments** about gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, appearance, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or nationality
- **Sexual language or imagery** in any context
- **Deliberate intimidation** or stalking
- **Harassing photography** or recording
- **Sustained disruption** of discussions or events
- **Discriminatory jokes** or slurs

### üö´ **Unprofessional Conduct**

- **Personal attacks** or insults
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing private information** without consent
- **Threats of violence** or harm
- **Spamming** or trolling
- **Intentional misinformation** that could harm patients

### üö´ **Healthcare-Specific Violations**

- **Sharing patient data** without proper authorization
- **Violating HIPAA** or other healthcare privacy regulations
- **Providing medical advice** without proper qualifications
- **Disregarding patient safety** protocols
- **Misrepresenting healthcare standards** or regulations

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it immediately:

### üìß **Email Reporting**

- **Primary Contact**: <conduct@zarishsphere.com>
- **Alternative**: <support@zarishsphere.com>
- **Confidentiality**: All reports will be kept confidential
- **Response Time**: Within 24 hours

### üêõ **GitHub Reporting**

- **Private Issues**: Create a private issue mentioning @conduct-team
- **Direct Messages**: Send a direct message to project maintainers
- **Anonymous Reporting**: Use our anonymous form at [https://forms.zarishsphere.com/conduct](https://forms.zarishsphere.com/conduct)

### üì± **Emergency Reporting**

For immediate threats or dangerous situations:

- **Contact local authorities** if there's immediate danger
- **Emergency Email**: <emergency@zarishsphere.com>
- **24/7 Hotline**: +1-555-ZARISH (available globally)

### What to Include in Your Report

When making a report, please include:

- **Your contact information** (optional, for follow-up)
- **Description of the incident** with dates and times
- **Individuals involved** (if known and comfortable sharing)
- **Any witnesses** or evidence (screenshots, messages, etc.)
- **Impact on you** or others
- **Desired resolution** (if any)

### Investigation Process

1. **Immediate Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
2. **Initial Assessment**: Within 48 hours
3. **Investigation**: Within 5 business days
4. **Resolution**: Within 10 business days
5. **Follow-up**: Within 30 days

## Enforcement Guidelines

### Response Actions

Based on the severity and nature of the violation, responses may include:

### ‚ö†Ô∏è **Minor Violations**

- **Private warning** with clear explanation
- **Request for public apology** or clarification
- **Temporary suspension** from certain community spaces
- **Required participation** in harassment prevention training

### üö´ **Moderate Violations**

- **Temporary ban** from community participation (1-4 weeks)
- **Removal of offensive content**
- **Public acknowledgment** of the violation
- **Mandatory compliance training**

### üö´ **Serious Violations**

- **Permanent ban** from all community spaces
- **Removal of all contributions** if appropriate
- **Reporting to authorities** if illegal activity occurred
- **Notification to relevant professional organizations**

### Appeals Process

If you believe a decision was made in error:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Send written appeal to <appeals@zarishsphere.com> within 7 days
2. **Review**: Different team members will review the case
3. **Decision**: Final decision within 5 business days
4. **Resolution**: All decisions are final and binding

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

### üí¨ **Online Communication**

- **Be respectful** in all digital interactions
- **Use appropriate language** for professional healthcare context
- **Avoid sarcasm** that could be misinterpreted
- **Consider tone** and cultural differences
- **Take conflicts to private channels** when appropriate

### üé• **Video and Voice Calls**

- **Mute when not speaking** to reduce background noise
- **Use professional backgrounds** or blur when needed
- **Be on time** for scheduled meetings
- **Dress appropriately** for healthcare professional context
- **Respect different time zones** when scheduling

### üìù **Documentation and Code**

- **Follow coding standards** and best practices
- **Write clear, helpful commit messages**
- **Include proper documentation** with contributions
- **Respect intellectual property** and licensing
- **Test thoroughly** before submitting changes

### Healthcare-Specific Guidelines

### üè• **Patient Privacy and Data Security**

- **Never share patient information** without proper authorization
- **Follow HIPAA, GDPR, and other relevant regulations**
- **Use de-identified data** in examples and discussions
- **Report any potential privacy breaches** immediately
- **Maintain professional boundaries** at all times

### üî¨ **Medical Advice and Expertise**

- **Do not provide medical advice** unless properly qualified
- **Clearly state qualifications** when sharing healthcare insights
- **Encourage consultation** with appropriate healthcare professionals
- **Respect different medical practices** across cultures and regions
- **Stay within scope** of your expertise and role

### üåç **International Healthcare Context**

- **Respect different healthcare systems** and practices
- **Be mindful of cultural differences** in healthcare delivery
- **Consider resource limitations** in different regions
- **Adapt solutions** for local healthcare contexts
- **Learn from international best practices**

## Conflict Resolution

### Step-by-Step Process

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ **Direct Communication**

- **Address concerns directly** with the individual first
- **Use "I" statements** to express feelings and concerns
- **Focus on behavior**, not personality
- **Be specific** about what was problematic
- **Listen to their perspective** without interruption

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Mediation**

- **Request mediation** from a community leader or maintainer
- **Choose a neutral mediator** respected by both parties
- **Set ground rules** for the mediation process
- **Focus on finding common ground** and solutions
- **Document agreements** and follow-up actions

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ **Formal Resolution**

- **Submit formal complaint** to the conduct team
- **Provide evidence** and specific examples
- **Participate in good faith** in the resolution process
- **Accept outcomes** that are fair and reasonable
- **Move forward constructively** after resolution

- **Regular check-ins** with community members

### üìä **Monitoring and Evaluation**

## Special Considerations

### üåê **Virtual and Remote Participation**

### Time Zone Awareness

- **Schedule meetings** at times that work for most participants
- **Record meetings** for those who cannot attend live
- **Use asynchronous communication** when possible
- **Be patient** with response time differences
- **Rotate meeting times** to accommodate different regions

### Language and Accessibility

- **Use clear, simple language** in international settings
- **Provide translations** when possible and needed
- **Use accessibility tools** for participants with disabilities
- **Be patient** with language barriers
- **Offer alternative communication** methods when needed

### üë• **Different Roles and Responsibilities**

### Project Maintainers

- **Model positive behavior** in all interactions
- **Address violations** promptly and fairly
- **Provide training** and guidance to community members
- **Maintain confidentiality** in sensitive situations
- **Seek help** when dealing with complex issues

### Healthcare Professionals

- **Maintain professional boundaries** at all times
- **Share expertise** while being approachable
- **Respect different levels** of technical knowledge
- **Provide context** for healthcare-specific decisions
- **Mentor others** in healthcare technology

### Technical Contributors

- **Consider healthcare impact** of technical decisions
- **Explain technical concepts** in accessible ways
- **Collaborate with healthcare professionals** on domain-specific issues
- **Prioritize patient safety** and data security
- **Document technical decisions** clearly

## Consequences and Enforcement

### Immediate Actions

### üö® **Emergency Situations**

For immediate threats or dangerous behavior:

- **Remove the individual** from community spaces immediately
- **Contact appropriate authorities** if necessary
- **Protect potential victims** from further harm
- **Preserve evidence** for investigation
- **Communicate with affected parties** appropriately

### ‚ö° **Clear Violations**

For obvious violations of this code:

- **Issue immediate warning** or temporary suspension
- **Remove offensive content** promptly
- **Notify relevant parties** of the action taken
- **Begin investigation** process
- **Document all actions** taken

### Long-term Consequences

### üìà **Pattern of Behavior**

For repeated violations:

- **Escalate consequences** with each incident
- **Require participation** in training programs
- **Implement monitoring** of future behavior
- **Consider permanent removal** from community
- **Share information** with other open source projects if appropriate

### üîÑ **Rehabilitation and Reintegration**

For those who demonstrate genuine change:

- **Provide pathways** for reintegration
- **Offer mentorship** and support
- **Recognize positive changes** publicly
- **Assign community service** opportunities
- **Monitor progress** and provide feedback
